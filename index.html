TRAVEL WRITING FROM NORTH AFRICA
United by common cultures, religions, and aridity, the countries of North Africa are sometimes known as The Dry World. Separated from the rest of Africa by the Sahara, and from Europe and the Middle East by water, the dry world remains a distinct and vivid place in the world. In these notes about traveling in Morocco, and beyond, I write about food, the history of cheese, and the idea of personal themes of travel. Follow me through medinas, courtyards and desert landscapes of the Dry World.
